## Risk Mitigation

### Risk 1: Fragmentation Strategy Failure
**Risk**: Reader experiences chaos instead of puzzle
**Mitigation**:
- Early beta reading of first 5 chapters
- Explicit calibration phase before proceeding past chapter 20
- Willingness to add "guide rails" if needed

### Risk 2: Acts I-III Scope Creep
**Risk**: Internal journey becomes bloated, losing narrative momentum
**Mitigation**:
- Set firm chapter count limit upfront (recommend 15-20 chapters)
- Each chapter must advance therapeutic arc AND contain essential plot information
- Regular structural reviews to cut chapters that don't earn their place

### Risk 3: Loss of Philosophical Depth
**Risk**: In pursuit of narrative flow, philosophical rigor is diluted
**Mitigation**:
- Maintain metaphysical integration checklist adherence
- Regular consultation with Strategic Narrative Plan
- Expert review of key philosophical chapters

### Risk 4: Multiple Planning Layer Conflicts
**Risk**: Contradictions between planning documents create inconsistencies
**Mitigation**:
- Complete "Unified Chapter Map" early (Short-term Goal 2.1)
- Designate Strategic Narrative Plan as ultimate arbiter
- Document and resolve all conflicts before major writing phases

## Notes for Implementation

### On the "Strategy of Fragmentation"

The Strategic Narrative Plan makes clear this is both the novel's greatest strength and risk. Key implementation principles:

1. **Each fragment must be inherently interesting** - Not just a puzzle piece, but a compelling mini-narrative
2. **Clues must be discoverable** - "Preference for symmetry" and "echoes of origin" should be detectable on careful reading
3. **Assembly should feel rewarding** - Reader should experience joy of connection, not frustration of obscurity
4. **Trust the reader** - Resist urge to over-explain, but provide sufficient context

### On Philosophical Integration

Per section 3.1 of Strategic Narrative Plan, concepts must be dramatized as events:

- **Don't write**: "AEGIS faced the Hard Problem of Consciousness"
- **Do write**: Show AEGIS processing the fragment's suffering signals, categorizing them as system errors, attempting and failing to understand the qualia

### On Character as Philosophy

Per section 3.2:
- **AEGIS** = Coherence Theory of Truth (internal consistency at all costs)
- **System Kael** = Correspondence Theory of Truth (alignment with external reality)

This philosophical conflict must be the engine of every major plot point.
