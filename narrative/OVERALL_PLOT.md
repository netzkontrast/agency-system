## Tone & Style

### Overall Tone
- **Philosophical Horror**: The terror is not visceral but intellectual
- **Cosmic Awe**: The scale is incomprehensible, the stakes infinite
- **Existential Melancholy**: Beauty and tragedy intertwined
- **Meditative Urgency**: Contemplative but with mounting dread

### Narrative Voice
- **First-Person (AEGIS/Fragment)**: Subjective, immediate, intimate
  - Used for experiential passages
  - Creates empathy and identification
  - Shows the "inside" of consciousness

- **Third-Person (Objective)**: Analytical, distant, scientific
  - Used for exposition and scientific context
  - Creates intellectual framework
  - Shows the "outside" view

- **Second-Person (Reader Address)**: Direct, challenging, invitational
  - Used sparingly in philosophical interludes
  - Breaks the fourth wall
  - Forces reader to confront their own assumptions

### Stylistic Choices
- **Short, fragmented sentences** during moments of crisis
- **Long, flowing prose** during moments of wonder or meditation
- **Technical precision** when discussing science
- **Poetic ambiguity** when discussing subjective experience
- **Silence and space** as narrative tools (white space, ellipses)
