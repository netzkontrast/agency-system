## Principles Extracted for Future Writing

Based on the Strategic Narrative Plan recommendations, the following principles should guide future chapter writing:

### Principle 1: Perspective Integrity
**DO**: Use experiential, subjective language from fragment's limited POV
**DON'T**: Use meta-conceptual terms (like "simulation") that the fragment couldn't know
**Example**: Instead of "I realized I might be in a simulation," write "An echo... of a broken symmetry, a logic that does not apply here"

### Principle 2: Show, Don't Tell Themes
**DO**: Let reader experience themes through narrative events
**DON'T**: Write authorial essays explaining what the subsequent chapter will show
**Example**: Delete any section that says "The next part will explore..." or "This represents..."

### Principle 3: Technical Term Precision
**DO**: Use technically precise terms when they carry thematic weight
**RETAIN**: "autopoietic" (self-creating), "dekoh√§renz" (decoherence), "Hilbert-Raum" (Hilbert space)
**DON'T**: Use jargon for jargon's sake or when simpler terms suffice

### Principle 4: Fragmentation as Feature
**DO**: Embed discoverable clues ("preference for symmetry", "echoes of origin")
**DO**: Reward attentive reading with "click" moments of connection
**DON'T**: Make fragments so obscure they become frustrating rather than puzzling
