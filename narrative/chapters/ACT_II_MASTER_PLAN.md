## Psychological Framework: Structural Dissociation

### Theory of Structural Dissociation (Van der Hart, Nijenhuis, Steele)

**Core Concept**: Trauma causes consciousness to fragment into distinct subsystems:

1. **ANP (Apparently Normal Parts)**
   - Handle daily functioning
   - Avoid trauma content
   - Present a "normal" face to the world
   - Focused on future/survival

2. **EP (Emotional Parts)**
   - Hold traumatic memories
   - Stuck in traumatic time
   - Defensive/protective functions
   - Focused on past/danger

3. **Dissociative Barriers**
   - Amnesia between parts
   - Distinct sense of self in each part
   - Communication difficulties
   - Phobias of trauma content, other parts, and integration

### Application to Narrative

- **AEGIS as Trauma Source**: The original traumatic event that caused System Kael's fragmentation
- **Parts as Survival**: Each part emerged to handle different aspects of existence under AEGIS
- **The Journey**: From fragmented survival → recognition → cooperation → (Act III) integration
