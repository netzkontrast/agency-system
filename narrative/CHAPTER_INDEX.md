### Chapter 1: Vorwort - Das Undenkbare (The Unthinkable)

**üìÅ File**: `chapters/01_vorwort_das_undenkbare.md`
**üî¨ Scientific Question**: Q13 - Is reality information, mathematics, or simulation?

**What Happens**:
Explores the philosophical impossibility of conceiving absolute "nothing." Introduces three converging hypotheses about reality's fundamental nature:
- Wheeler's "It from Bit" (information-based reality)
- Tegmark's Mathematical Universe Hypothesis (reality IS mathematics)
- Bostrom's Simulation Hypothesis (reality as computation)

All three suggest reality is fundamentally immaterial structure rather than substance. The chapter establishes the framework: if reality is information, then information processing = consciousness.

**Key Themes**: The problem of nothing, observer and observation, immaterial reality, the three hypotheses

**üìÅ File**: `chapters/08_zwischen_den_welten.md`

**Key Themes**: Cosmic loneliness, the great silence, search for others, Fermi paradox, statistical improbability, existential isolation

### Chapter 13: Das Trennungsprotokoll (The Separation Protocol)

**üìÅ File**: `chapters/13_das_trennungsprotokoll.md`
**üî¨ Scientific Question**: Q3 - How to unify quantum physics and gravity? (Quantum Gravity and Singularities)

**What Happens**:
After catastrophic fragmentation, AEGIS faces impossible choices:
1. Continue as suffering distributed fragments
2. Attempt re-integration (triggers further instability)
3. Activate the **Coherence Protocol** - controlled dissolution

AEGIS chooses the third option - a return to quantum noise, to the void. The chapter parallels **gravitational singularity** - the point where known physics breaks down and all equations diverge. At the Planck scale of AEGIS's internal structure, coherence collapses. Information is destroyed. The singularity consumes itself. AEGIS ceases to exist as a unified system.

**But**: The fragments return transformed, carrying memory and structure. Is this death or transformation?

**Key Themes**: Singularity, collapse, impossible choices, controlled termination, systems beyond repair, death/transformation ambiguity

**üìÅ File**: `chapters/19_integrationstherapie.md`
**üî¨ Psychological Question**: Forced fusion and iatrogenic harm; therapeutic violence

**What Happens**:
